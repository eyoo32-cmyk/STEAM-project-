Rolling time on a wooden ramp

  • Introduction: We decided to conduct this experiment out of curiosity about which type of surface produces the most friction.
  • The aim of this investigation is to explore how the surface material of a ball affects the time it takes to roll down a ramp.

  • o test this, we will build a cardboard ramp at a fixed angle and release balls made of different materials (wood, metal, rubber, glass, and plastic) from the same height.
  • We will measure the time taken for each ball to reach the bottom of the ramp.

  • By comparing these times, we can determine how surface texture and material influence friction and motion and if it affects the time.
  • This experiment will help us better understand how friction affects speed and acceleration in real-life situations.
